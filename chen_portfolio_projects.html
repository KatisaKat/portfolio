Featured Work

RKY Camp is a summer camp held in Kingston, Ontario. It is friendly to kids of all
ages, and hold numerous different events over the seasons. Despite being very
reputable and trustworthy, it’s original site does not convey or maximize its
individuality.

In my redesign of the RKY Camp website, I retained the original green, yellow, and
red color scheme but muted the saturation to make it more visually comfortable.
The contrast was already very nice in the original design, but it was slightly too
harsh and bright. In my previous feedback, I noted the cluttered layout of the
original site, so I introduced more whitespace and improved organization to create
a sense of “breathing space.” I streamlined the content by removing lengthy text
and repositioning sections, placing items like “Health & Safety” under Facilities
instead of About. 

Furthermore, the original navigation bar was repeated three times on the
homepage, but I simplified it to just one instance, replacing the others with
concise references and an introductory message. I focused on including only
essential information on the main page, as it’s the first point of contact for visitors,
ensuring it remains easy to navigate and understand. I emphasized the
registration button, since it is a key feature of the site, and made the contact form
and introduction easily accessible.
